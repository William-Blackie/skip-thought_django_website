The use of summaries is an integral part of daily life whether it is used in the headlines of articles to the averages of sports scores of a player in each season. They help define whether the media presented is worth examining further, as well as providing a valuable context which otherwise would cause confusion. The use of summaries is fundamental to all media types. However, I will be focussing on the role of summarisation on text media, specifically news articles. In 2018, Ofcom found 64% of adults use the internet for news (Jigsaw Research, 2018, p. 13); with the Guardian being the most viewed online with 5,358 per a week (Jigsaw Research, 2018, p. 41), as well as 23% of all UK users used the BBC website/app for news (Jigsaw Research, 2018, p. 20). These findings show the vast utilisation of digital news media in UK and its reach to majority of the population therefore raises the need for more accurate summation of information due to vast number of users.
